Tokyo Land Enterprise Challenge
(The Japanese text follows.)

What to Build
Build one dashboard  that allows staff to quickly check the status of each member company.

The dashboard should display:

Company name
Contract status
Renewal date
Payment status
Invoice request status
Include a simple search function so staff can quickly find a member company.

Focus on one simple workflow — not a complete management system.


Do not try to build a complete system.

The goal is to show one clear workflow:

“Can staff quickly check what is happening with each member company?”


Why This Matters

Today, membership, contract, payment, and billing information is managed manually across multiple files and communication channels.

This makes it hard to quickly answer questions such as:

Which companies need contract renewal soon?
Which payments are completed or pending?
Which invoice requests are missing?
What is the latest status of this member company?
If this information can be managed and searched in one place, Sakura Deeptech Shibuya staff can reduce manual work, prevent missed renewals or billing requests, and operate with more accuracy and confidence.

Participation in the hackathon does not guarantee implementation or commercial engagement.

Scenario

A staff member receives a phone call from Company ABC asking about their contract status.

The staff member searches for "Company ABC" and immediately sees:

Contract: Active
Renewal Date: August 31, 2026
Payment Status: Paid
Invoice Request: Sent
The staff can answer the customer's question in seconds.

Tokyu Fudosan Enterprise Challenge

What to make
Please create a simple MVP dashboard that allows the Sakura Deeptech Shibuya operations staff to view information about member companies in a list format.

The information covered in the MVP section will be limited to the following:

Display items

Company name
Contract status
Update date
Payment status
Billing request status
* Searchable

There is no need to create a production system.

The goal of this project is to clearly illustrate the following single business process flow.

"Can staff quickly check the current status of each member company?"



Why is it important?

Currently, membership information, contracts, payments, and billing information are managed across multiple files and communication channels.

Therefore, the following verifications will take some time.

Which companies' contracts are due for renewal soon?
Which companies have completed payments and which have not yet completed payments?
Have any billing requests been missed?
What is the current status of one of our member companies?
By centralizing this information and making it readily searchable and verifiable, manual work can be reduced, updates and billing errors can be prevented, and facility operations can be made more accurate and reliable.
